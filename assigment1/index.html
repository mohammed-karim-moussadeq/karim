Exotic dance moves

Yes, you are right! As part of my post-doctorate work, I did study exotic tribal dances. To answer your question, my favourite dance are as follows, with definitions:

    polynesian chickens dance

A little known but very influential dance dating back as far as 300BC, a whole village would dance around in a circle like chickens, to encourage their lifestock to be "fruitful".

    Icelandic brownian shuffle

before the Icelanders developed fire as a means of getting warm, they used to practice, which involved huddling close together in a circle on the floor, and shuffling their bodies around in imperceptibilty tiny, very rapid movements. One of my fellow students used to say that he tought this dance inspired modern styles such as twerking.

    Arctic robot dance.

An interesting example of historic misinformation, English explorers in the 1960s believed to have discovered a new dance style characterised by "robotic", stilted movements, being practiced by inhabitants of Northern Alaska and Canada. Later on however it was discovered that they were just moving like this because they were really cold.
